Introduction

The 4th Edition of our DriveX Workshop explores the integration of foundation models and V2X-based cooperative systems to improve perception, planning, and decision-making in autonomous vehicles. While traditional single-vehicle systems have advanced tasks like 3D object detection, emerging challenges like holistic scene understanding and 3D occupancy prediction require more comprehensive solutions. Collaborative driving systems, utilizing V2X communication and roadside infrastructure, extend sensory range, provide hazard warnings, and improve decision-making through shared data. Simultaneously, foundation models like Vision-Language Models (VLMs) offer generalization abilities, enabling zero-shot learning, open-vocabulary recognition, and scene explanation for novel scenarios. Recent advancements in end-to-end systems and foundation models like DriveLLM further enhance autonomous systems. The workshop aims to bring together experts to explore these technologies, address challenges, and advance road safety.

Challenge

Challenge tracks: The details and objectives of each challenge track are as follows.

  • V2I-Based Cooperative Perception: This challenge will focus on perception fusion between vehicles and roadside infrastructure using the TUMTraf V2X CP dataset. The goal is to explore how infrastructure-mounted sensors (e.g., LiDAR, Radar, cameras) can provide complementary and context-rich information to enhance the perception capabilities of connected vehicles. The task centers on cooperative 3D object detection and tracking, aiming to quantify how infrastructure assistance can improve perception robustness, occlusion handling, and environmental awareness.

  • Accident Scene Understanding and Safety Reasoning: The second challenge is built upon the TUMTraf Accid3nD dataset and targets high-risk and accident-prone traffic scenarios. Participants will develop models that integrate infrastructure-based perception for early risk assessment and accident prediction. By evaluating how cooperative perception can identify and anticipate critical safety events, this challenge aims to push the boundary of AI systems in proactive safety reasoning, which is an essential step toward Vision-Zero mobility goals.

  • End-to-End Multi-Agent Autonomous Driving: The third challenge extends to vision-based cooperative planning using two real-world datasets V2XPnP and V2V4Real. Participants will design models that perform trajectory planning either from single-vehicle inputs, multi-vehicle shared features, or combined vehicle‚Äìinfrastructure information. This track aims to bridge perception and decision-making through cooperative intelligence, encouraging research into how real-world V2X communication can enhance driving policy learning, coordination efficiency, and safety in complex dynamic scenarios.
